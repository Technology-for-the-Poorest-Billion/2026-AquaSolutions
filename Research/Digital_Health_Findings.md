# Digital Health in Zimbabwe

## Digital Health Solutions for Non-Communicable Diseases in Zimbabwe
- Source: https://www.unicefusa.org/stories/digital-health-solutions-non-communicable-disease-care-zimbabwe
- VHWs use digital medical equipment and smartphones to assist in their testing, screening, and treatment procedures.
- Prior to this digitilisation, VHWs would record patient notes and then transmit these to the public healthcare system after completing their journey, introducing significant delays in the analysis of the results. This was often done using paper reports compiled into books. 
- Now, VHWs collect information on their phone which they send to the nearest clinic at the end of the day.
- Digital platforms also provide VHWs on instructions on how to proceed based on some readings they make (e.g. temperature, blood pressure).
- VHWs are all equipped with phones (some provided by UNICEF).
- Uploading this data so quickly also allows clinics to prepare in advance of a patient arriving at their centre. This speeds up the "lead time" for treatment.

## New Digital Tool Will Streamline COVID Data Collection and Analysis in Zimbabwe
- Source: https://pedaids.org/resource/new-digital-tool-will-streamline-covid-data-collection-and-analysis-in-zimbabwe/
- The FHIR (Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources) adapter can be used to integrate a service into Impilo.
- Two one-day training sessions were used for 43 workers across different clinics.
- For returning clinic patients, their demographic data and previous records are managed so that they can be accessed easily.
- The seven facilities in this trial were equipped with mobile data support.
- They used the DHIS2 Android client.

## DHIS2 Android: Extending the Reach of DHIS2
- Source: https://dhis2.org/android-in-action/
- Historically, medical data was collected on paper reports which were then submitted to a central office for manual data entry.
- DHIS2 de-centralises this process. It is a web-based application where anyone in a system, ranging from the Minister of Health to an employee at a health clinic, can enter data. Via the app, employees can directly upload data to the healthcare system.
- The app works in offline mode, allowing users to enter data which is uploaded upon access to the internet.
- The app's official title is DHIS2 Android Capture.
- Capabilities: Data entry, real-time analysis, search existing records, map view, secure login, QR code scanning.
